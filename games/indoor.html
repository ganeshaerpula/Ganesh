Indoor Games

Indoor games, within the controlled setting of the home, offered unparalleled opportunities for adults, whether parents, caregivers, or educators, to direct children's play. Historian Jay Mechling has observed that adults began trying to shape play into a constructive form in the nineteenth century, often through toys. The kindergarten movement, based on a system of early childhood education developed by Friedrich Froebel in the 1840s, advocated learning through play, especially in early childhood.
